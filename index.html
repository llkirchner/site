Three Rivers
Mysteries

When reality has “versions” who do you trust?

Each Three Rivers Mystery drops you into a different corner of 1990s Pittsburgh — from boardrooms to back rooms to performance spaces — where stories are easily rewritten and the wrong woman always ends up in the crosshairs. The catch: that woman isn't always sure she can trust herself. At first. But when she finally decides to believe herself, she becomes the most dangerous person in the room. For readers who love an underdog. Think office politics, family dysfunction, and a lot of bad choices as my heroines discover that reality isn't a given. Because writing contemporary issues through a historical lens lets me get away with a lot.

A notorious theater critic is poisoned and all eyes turn to the woman he replaced — a broke Gen X freelancer whose mental health history makes her doubt her own innocence. To clear her name she launches a desperate investigation, but can she untangle the artistic rivalries, false personas, and buried secrets before she’s next?

Who Am I To Write About Rebellious Women?

I've been a journalist, a story coach, an award-winning screenwriter, and even an on-air host for the Home Shopping Network. My life and work as an expat in Asia became the basis of two memoirs. And I was once simultaneously the religion editor for an LGBTQIA+ paper, the dating columnist for an alt newsweekly, and the bridal editor for a society rag.

My writing has appeared in The Washington Post, Salon, and The Rumpus among numerous other outlets.

I’m also a proud grandma, longtime yoga teacher, and avid garden lady, living in Florida with my favorite husband and our best boy, Hartley.

I’ve never believed the most powerful person in the room is the noisiest, but the one who’s paying attention.
